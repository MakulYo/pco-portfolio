Why ICT fits me

ICT suits me because it combines problem-solving with creativity. I enjoy looking at a complex issue, breaking it down into smaller steps, and building a structured solution. At the same time, I like that ICT leaves room for experimenting and coming up with original ideas — it's not just about following instructions but also about finding new approaches. In my past work as a game tester, I learned to spot small issues and think critically about how systems behave. That same mindset is useful in programming: being able to track down where something goes wrong and fixing it step by step.

Another reason is the direct impact. ICT skills are used everywhere: from the apps we use daily to the systems that keep businesses running. Knowing that what I learn here can be applied to real projects and make a difference motivates me. It means that the effort I put into studying pays off in a visible way.

Finally, this study suits me because it keeps me challenged. I know I will have to stay consistent with my time and effort to keep up. That pressure motivates me to improve my discipline, and I like the idea that every finished project is proof of growth. ICT matches not only my interests but also the personal skills I want to strengthen for the future.
